Book a quarantine-free break in Abu Dhabi

Updated 7 February 2021, if you’re travelling on a direct flight from any of the countries or regions listed below, or you’re taking part in the National Vaccination Programme in the UAE and travelling from any of the countries or regions listed below, you’ll be exempt from quarantine requirements when you arrive:

  • Australia
  • Bhutan
  • Brunei
  • China
  • Greenland
  • Hong Kong region (China)
  • Iceland
  • Mauritius
  • Mongolia
  • New Zealand
  • Saudi Arabia
  • Singapore

You must take a COVD-19 PCR test on day 6 and day 12 of your arrival into Abu Dhabi. If you’re part of the National Vaccination Programme you’ll only need to take a COVID-19 PCR test on day 6. Read more.

Regulations for flying to Abu Dhabi

Always check the latest travel regulations before you fly. You must have a COVID-19 PCR test 96 hours before your flight, or 72 hours before if you’re flying from the UK, and you may be required to self-isolate for 10 days. If you're transferring in Abu Dhabi, the maximum connection time is 12 hours.

Travelling to Dubai or another emirate

If you are travelling to another emirate, including Dubai, you will simply need to follow the quarantine rules of that emirate. You will only be permitted to travel to another emirate by Etihad Chauffeur, Etihad Express Coach or Airport Taxi.
